# Recommendation: 4-Tier Harmonized Contributor System

**Date**: 2025-11-03
**Status**: Alternative Proposal (4-Tier vs 3-Tier)
**Approval Needed**: Plone Framework Team, Foundation Board

---

## Executive Summary

This document proposes a **4-tier system** for both PLIP and PR contributions, providing more granular recognition than the 3-tier alternative while maintaining harmonization between strategic and implementation work.

**Key Feature**: Separate thresholds for PLIPs and PRs that create equivalent recognition tiers based on community role and impact.

---

## Proposed 4-Tier System

### Tier Structure with Dual Tracks

| Tier | Name | Strategic Track (PLIPs) | Implementation Track (PRs) | Badge |
|------|------|-------------------------|----------------------------|-------|

## Key Advantages of 4-Tier System

### 1. More Granular Recognition

**vs 3-Tier System**:
- Separates "Elite" (Level 1) from "Leaders" (Level 2)
- Distinguishes exceptional long-term contributors
- Creates aspirational top tier

**Benefit**:
- tisto (61 PLIPs) gets special recognition vs 10-19 PLIP contributors
- Celebrates true legends of the community

### 2. Better Mid-Tier Clarity

**Level 2 (Leaders)** vs **Level 3 (Active)**:
- Level 2: 10-19 PLIPs = established leaders (5+ years)
- Level 3: 3-9 PLIPs = active contributors (2-5 years)
- Clearer distinction than lumping both as "Gold"

### 3. Natural Statistical Breaks

**PLIP Thresholds**:
- 20+ → Elite (3 people) - clear outliers
- 10-19 → Leaders (5 people) - sustained excellence
- 3-9 → Active (16 people) - engaged contributors
- 1-2 → Emerging (39 people) - newcomers

**PR Thresholds**:
- 500+ → Elite (3 people) - exceptional volume
- 200-499 → Leaders (3 people) - sustained high output
- 50-199 → Active (20 people) - regular contributors
- 1-49 → Emerging (352 people) - broad base

### 4. Comparable Tier Sizes at Top

| Tier | PLIP Contributors | PR Contributors | Total Unique |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **💎 Level 1** | 3 | 3 | **6** (sneridagh in both) |

## Comparison: 3-Tier vs 4-Tier Systems

### 3-Tier System (Platinum/Gold/Silver)

| Tier | PLIPs | PRs | Total People |
|------|-------|-----|--------------|
| Platinum | 10+ (8 people) | 300+ (12 people) | ~16-20 |
| Gold | 3-9 (22 people) | 100-299 (17 people) | ~32-40 |
| Silver | 1-2 (33 people) | 1-99 (353 people) | ~370+ |

**Pros**:
- ✅ Simpler (3 levels easier to understand)
- ✅ Larger Platinum tier (more recognition)
- ✅ Easier to communicate

**Cons**:
- ❌ Merges elite (20+) with leaders (10-19)
- ❌ Loses nuance at top tier
- ❌ Less aspirational peak

### 4-Tier System (Elite/Leaders/Active/Emerging)

| Tier | PLIPs | PRs | Total People |
|------|-------|-----|--------------|
| Level 1 | 20+ (3 people) | 500+ (3 people) | ~6 |
| Level 2 | 10-19 (5 people) | 200-499 (3 people) | ~8 |
| Level 3 | 3-9 (16 people) | 50-199 (20 people) | ~36 |
| Level 4 | 1-2 (39 people) | 1-49 (352 people) | ~391 |

**Pros**:
- ✅ More granular recognition
- ✅ Separates elite from leaders
- ✅ Better mid-tier distinction
- ✅ More aspirational peak (Level 1)

**Cons**:
- ❌ More complex (4 levels)
- ❌ Smaller top tiers (may feel exclusive)
- ❌ Slightly harder to communicate

## Why 4 Tiers Work Better

### Elite Recognition Matters

**Problem with 3-tier**: tisto (61 PLIPs) and jensens (11 PLIPs) in same tier
- Vastly different contribution levels
- Doesn't recognize exceptional outliers

**Solution with 4-tier**: Separate Level 1 for exceptional contributors
- Level 1: 20+ PLIPs (3 people) / 500+ PRs (3 people)
- Level 2: 10-19 PLIPs (5 people) / 200-499 PRs (3 people)
- Both get high recognition, but elite get highest

### Mid-Tier Clarity

**Problem with 3-tier**: Gold combines 3-9 PLIPs (varies 3x)
- 3 PLIPs = just starting serious engagement
- 9 PLIPs = nearly at leadership level

**Solution with 4-tier**: Split into two meaningful groups
- Level 2: 10-19 PLIPs = proven leaders
- Level 3: 3-9 PLIPs = active but not yet leaders
- Clearer progression path

### Community Structure

4 tiers better reflect actual community structure:
- **Level 1**: The legends (founding architects, long-term visionaries)
- **Level 2**: The core team (sustained leaders)
- **Level 3**: The active community (regular contributors)
- **Level 4**: The pipeline (newcomers and emerging voices)

## Threshold Justification

### Why 20 PLIPs for Elite?

**Data**:
- Only 3 people have 20+ PLIPs
- Natural break: 20, 30, 61 vs 11, 11, 16, 17, 17
- Represents true exceptional outliers

**Impact**:
- tisto: 61 PLIPs = 20% of all PLIPs
- sneridagh: 30 PLIPs = 10% of all PLIPs
- bloodbare: 20 PLIPs (intense period)

### Why 10 PLIPs for Leaders?

**Data**:
- Natural clustering: 5 people at 10-19
- Separates sustained excellence (10+) from active (3-9)

**Impact**:
- All have 5+ years engagement
- Recognized community leaders
- Mentors to others

### Why 500 PRs (3yr) for Elite?

**Data**:
- Only 3 people have 500+ PRs
- Equivalent to 167+ PRs/year sustained
- Exceptional commitment

**Comparison**:
- Roughly equivalent effort to 20+ PLIPs over time
- Both require many years of dedication

### Why 200 PRs (3yr) for Leaders?

**Data**:
- Small group: 3 people at 200-499
- Equivalent to 67-167 PRs/year
- Sustained high output

**Creates balance**:
- 3 PR Leaders + 5 PLIP Leaders = 8 total Level 2
- 3 PR Elite + 3 PLIP Elite = 6 total Level 1
- Similar tier sizes

## Progression Paths

### Strategic Track (PLIPs)

```
Level 4 (1-2 PLIPs)
    ↓ +1-2 PLIPs
Level 3 (3-9 PLIPs)
    ↓ +1-7 PLIPs (reach 10)
Level 2 (10-19 PLIPs)
    ↓ +1-10 PLIPs (reach 20)
Level 1 (20+ PLIPs) - Elite
```

**Timeline**: Typically 5-10+ years from Level 4 to Level 1

### Implementation Track (PRs)

```
Level 4 (1-49 PRs/3yr)
    ↓ Reach 50 PRs over 3 years
Level 3 (50-199 PRs/3yr)
    ↓ Sustain 67+ PRs/year for 3 years (200 total)
Level 2 (200-499 PRs/3yr)
    ↓ Sustain 167+ PRs/year for 3 years (500 total)
Level 1 (500+ PRs/3yr) - Elite
```

**Timeline**: Can advance faster (1-3 years from 4 to 3, 2-5 years total to Level 1 for prolific contributors)

### Multi-Track Achievement

**Balanced Contributors** can qualify at different levels:
- **sneridagh**: Level 1 Strategic + Level 1 Implementation = 💎💎 (Double Elite)
- **mauritsvanrees**: Level 2 Strategic + Level 1 Implementation = 🏆💎
- **petschki**: Level 3 Strategic + Level 2 Implementation = ⭐🏆

**Highest Recognition**: Level 1 in both tracks (💎💎) - Currently only sneridagh

## Comparison Summary: 3-Tier vs 4-Tier

### Choose 3-Tier If:
- ✅ Simplicity is paramount
- ✅ Want larger top tier (Platinum = 8+12 = ~20 people)
- ✅ Easier initial communication
- ✅ Less intimidating hierarchy

### Choose 4-Tier If:
- ✅ Want to recognize exceptional outliers separately
- ✅ Need clearer mid-tier distinction
- ✅ Value granular progression path
- ✅ Want more aspirational peak (Elite status)

---

## Recommendation

We recommend the **4-tier system** because:

1. ✅ **Recognizes True Legends**: Separates elite (20+ PLIPs, 500+ PRs) from leaders
2. ✅ **Natural Breaks**: Statistical clustering supports these thresholds
3. ✅ **Community Structure**: Better reflects actual contribution patterns
4. ✅ **Aspirational**: Level 1 (Elite) creates meaningful peak to strive for
5. ✅ **Manageable Complexity**: 4 tiers not significantly more complex than 3
6. ✅ **Mid-Tier Clarity**: Level 2 vs Level 3 is clearer than single "Gold"

**Bottom Line**: The 4-tier system provides better granularity without excessive complexity, and properly celebrates exceptional long-term contributors.
